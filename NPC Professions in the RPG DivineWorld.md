# Core Civilization Jobs

## Leader / Chief

The highest authority of a tribe.

Responsibilities:

- Make major tribal decisions.
- Decide whether to relocate settlements.
- Declare wars.
- Negotiate peace.
- Assign important responsibilities.
- Decide expansion priorities.
- Coordinate large construction projects.
- Resolve disputes between NPCs.

The leader wasn't supposed to micromanage individuals but rather guide the civilization.

---

## Farmer

Responsible for food production.

Tasks included:

- Finding fertile land.
- Planting crops.
- Harvesting.
- Replanting.
- Maintaining irrigation (if available later).
- Storing surplus food.
- Feeding the village during shortages.

Food production was one of the foundations of civilization.

---

## Miner

Collected underground resources.

Responsibilities:

- Mining ores.
- Collecting stone.
- Expanding mines.
- Avoiding dangerous caves.
- Reporting valuable discoveries.
- Supplying blacksmiths and builders.

---

## Builder

Constructed civilization infrastructure.

Responsibilities:

- Houses.
- Roads.
- Farms.
- Defensive walls.
- Storage buildings.
- Bridges.
- Public structures.

Builders later used blueprint-based construction instead of random block placement.

---

## Hunter

Obtained food and animal products.

Responsibilities:

- Hunting passive mobs.
- Tracking animals.
- Bringing meat.
- Leather collection.
- Population management of wildlife.

---

## Lumberjack

Responsible for wood.

Tasks:

- Cutting trees.
- Replanting saplings.
- Managing forests.
- Supplying builders.

---

## Explorer

One of my favorite professions.

Responsibilities:

- Travel beyond known territory.
- Discover new biomes.
- Locate villages.
- Locate structures.
- Remember dangerous areas.
- Return home.
- Share discoveries.

Explorers expanded civilization knowledge.

---

## Cartographer

Worked closely with explorers.

Responsibilities:

- Create maps.
- Update maps.
- Merge exploration reports.
- Maintain geographical records.
- Build the Great Map Library.

Rather than discovering locations, they documented them.

---

## Trader

Managed economic exchange.

Responsibilities:

- Trading with villages.
- Trading with other tribes.
- Bartering.
- Estimating value.
- Transporting goods.

They were responsible for keeping resources flowing between civilizations.

---

## Diplomat

Responsible for inter-civilization relations.

Tasks:

- Peace negotiations.
- Alliance proposals.
- Trade agreements.
- Conflict resolution.
- Delivering official messages.

Instead of armies always fighting, diplomacy could prevent wars.

---

## Priest

Responsible for religion.

Tasks:

- Lead rituals.
- Spread beliefs.
- Maintain temples.
- Increase faith.
- Interpret divine events.
- Teach religious traditions.

This profession became important once Gods entered the simulation.

---

## Librarian

Keeper of civilization knowledge.

Responsibilities:

- Store books.
- Organize written knowledge.
- Preserve old writings.
- Help NPCs access information.

Libraries became civilization memory.

---

## Scribe

Responsible for writing.

Tasks:

- Copy books.
- Write discoveries.
- Record teachings.
- Preserve important information.

Without scribes, knowledge could disappear.

---

## The additional things scribe did

They would record things like:

- Births.
- Deaths.
- Marriages.
- Wars.
- Peace treaties.
- New settlements.
- Technological discoveries.
- Religious events.
- Natural disasters.
- Famous explorers.
- Heroes.
- Great builders.
- The rise and fall of tribes.

Those records would become books that future generations could read.

Those books can become part of the civilization's historical memory rather than simply disappearing.

---

## Storyteller

Their purpose was different from librarians.

Responsibilities included:

- Tell stories to NPCs.
- Spread legends.
- Preserve oral traditions.
- Increase morale.
- Keep famous events alive through speech.
- Turn history into culture.

Storytellers weren't just historians.

They were the civilization's culture.

---

## Blacksmith

Responsibilities:

- Smelting.
- Tool crafting.
- Weapon crafting.
- Armor production.
- Equipment repair.

As the civilization advanced they would produce better equipment.

---

## Fisherman

Responsibilities:

- Fishing.
- Food production.
- Exploring rivers.
- Providing aquatic resources.

---

## Brewer

Responsibilities:

- Brewing potions.
- Maintaining brewing stands.
- Producing medicine.
- Producing combat potions.

---

## Enchanter

Responsible for magical equipment.

Tasks:

- Enchanting tools.
- Managing enchantment tables.
- Choosing enchantments intelligently.

---

## Redstone Engineer

One of the advanced professions.

Responsibilities:

- Build redstone mechanisms.
- Automate farms.
- Design traps.
- Improve civilization efficiency.

# The important thing

An important design principle wasn't the professions themselves—it was **how NPCs got them**.

Originally, professions could have been assigned directly.

But over time, the philosophy changed to:

> **Professions should emerge because the civilization needs them.**

For example:

- If nobody maps the world, eventually someone becomes a Cartographer.
- If knowledge starts accumulating, someone naturally becomes a Librarian.
- If diplomacy becomes necessary, a Diplomat emerges.
- If many events occur, someone begins recording history.
- If religion spreads, Priests appear.

That shift—from fixed roles to **need-driven professions**—was one of the defining ideas behind DivineWorld's civilization simulation.
